# The Jamestown Logistics Manager (1607)

A text-based historical survival simulation that recreates the grueling, often tedious, and statistically perilous journey of the first permanent English settlement in the Americas.

## 📜 Project Overview
This game is designed to be **educational, historically grounded, and intentionally difficult.** It captures the struggle of the 104 original settlers who sailed on the *Susan Constant*, *Godspeed*, and *Discovery*. 

As the Expedition Leader, you must manage resources against the harsh realities of the 17th century: scurvy, brackish water, unrealistic corporate expectations from the London Company, and the complete absence of gold.

## 🚢 Features
* **The Atlantic Crossing:** Navigate the 3,000-mile voyage while managing rations and morale.
* **Historical Random Events:** Encounter scurvy outbreaks and variable weather conditions.
* **Settlement Management:** Once in Virginia, decide between the search for riches (Fool's Gold) or essential survival tasks like digging wells and planting corn.
* **The "Starving Time" Mechanic:** If food stores hit zero, the population declines rapidly.

## 🛠️ Installation
To play the game, you only need Python 3 installed on your system.

1.  **Clone the repository:**
    ```git clone [https://github.com/your-username/jamestown-logistics-manager.git](https://github.com/your-username/jamestown-logistics-manager.git)```
    
2.  **Navigate to the directory:**

    ```cd jamestown-logistics-manager```
    
3.  **Run the game:**
    ```python game.py```
    

## 🎮 How to Play
The game is played entirely through text prompts. 
* **Phase 1 (The Sea):** Focus on reaching the 3,000-mile mark. Tip: Don't ignore the morale of the crew, or they may lose the will to sail.
* **Phase 2 (The Land):** Focus on infrastructure. While the London Company wants gold, your settlers need fresh water and calories to survive the winter.

## 🎓 Educational Objectives
This simulation highlights several key historical truths:
* **Economic Misconceptions:** The settlers wasted valuable time looking for gold that didn't exist instead of securing food sources.
* **Geography:** The choice of Jamestown Island was strategically good for defense but ecologically disastrous due to the swampy, mosquito-infested terrain and brackish water.
* **Mortality:** In reality, only about 38 of the original 104 settlers survived the first year. Can you do better?
